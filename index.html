The problem

A common, rising disease feeding a queue no one can prioritise.

Osteoarthritis is the progressive "wear and tear" of joint cartilage, common, rising, and knee-dominated. It drives the largest elective queue in the NHS, yet at the point of referral there is no validated, equitable way to tell who will actually need surgery, so the clinic fills with patients who never needed it while the ones who do wait.

The patient journey

Most of the pathway is conservative care. One decision jams it.

From first symptoms to surgery, the patient passes through a long chain of primary and community care. The pinch point is a single, subjective decision.

The referral into secondary-care orthopaedics is subjective, with no validated tool. About 1 in 3 patients are discharged at the first visit and only ~28% convert to surgery, so the surgeon's clinic fills with people who never needed one.

KneeCast plugs in here — de-escalate the low-risk to conservative care, fast-track true progressors to the surgeon.

The evidence

Who triages a referral changes everything.

Surgical conversion rate, the share of clinic patients who actually proceed to surgery, swings dramatically with who does the triage.

A physio-led triage layer roughly doubles conversion versus a raw GP referral. First-contact triage manages ~87% of MSK patients in primary care, each avoided referral is worth ~£145–200, and one MSK triage service cut referrals ~30% for ~£1m in first-year savings. KneeCast is the predictive engine for that triage layer.

How it works

Routine clinical data in, a time-aware risk score out.

A survival model turns the information a clinician already has at the referral point into one number per knee, the risk and timing of progressing to surgery.

Baseline data

Pain and function scores, comorbidity, BMI, analgesic use, and, where available, the X-ray grade. No new tests.

Survival model

A time-to-event model learns who progresses, and how fast, from a large longitudinal cohort.

Risk & timing

Out comes a per-knee risk of replacement over one to five years, with an explanation of the drivers.

Triage decision

De-escalate the low-risk to conservative care, fast-track true progressors, all inside shared decision-making.

Why it's different

Accurate prediction is solved. Trustworthy, deployable prediction is not.

Models that predict knee replacement already exist. Almost none of them is fair across groups, portable to a new health system, or translated into a decision. That is the whole point of this project.

Fair

Performance is audited within subgroups, sex, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, and corrected, so accuracy holds for everyone, not just the average patient.

Portable

Trained on one population and tested on another, so we can show it survives the move to a different health system, not just a held-out split of the same data.

Actionable

The score is turned into referrals and scans avoided, and into a higher surgical conversion rate, an efficiency case a health system can actually cost.

Grounded in guidance, not data-dredging

NICE reviewed exactly this question and found no knee-specific prognostic model. KneeCast's features map onto the predictors NICE endorsed, pain and function scores, non-response to analgesics, symptom duration and X-ray grade, with age, BMI, comorbidity and deprivation as adjustments, and a survival design is the time-to-event method it called for.

Built for the clinic

A second opinion inside the record, not another app to open.

  • Embedded in the EHR. Appears as one panel in the patient file, alongside the data it already holds.
  • Explainable by design. Every score shows its main drivers and an uncertainty range, so a clinician can interrogate it.
  • Consistent over time. A retrained model does not silently re-triage a patient without new information.
The line we don't cross

Decision support, not a gate. Clinical guidance is explicit that scoring tools must not be used to decide who is eligible for a referral. KneeCast supports a shared decision and helps prioritise, it never denies anyone a referral.

Research prototype. Not a diagnostic device and not for clinical use.
